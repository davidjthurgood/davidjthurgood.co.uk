I've designed 3 routes of wireframes and then whittled my way down from there, including some higher fidelity mockups in an effort to increase my understanding of the brand and a responsive prototype.

View the HTML/CSS Prototype

Route One.

My goal here is to connect the login and sign up flows in a more obvious manner. Keeping it relatively compact on the page allows for the design to remain consistent across screen sizes and allows room for other brand/co-branding elements

Route Two.

Removing the constraints of a bounding area allows the form to look much cleaner at all screen sizes, larger input fields help clarity and allow more room for helpful elements, like a forgotten password tooltip and selection indicator.

Route Three.

I wanted to make the transition between Login and Sign up slicker, while all three transitions between these sections work in a similar way this allows the 'login' text to read like a title to the form, increasing clarity.

Route Four.

This route is a combination of the three other routes but primarily routes two and three. A centralised design which for me reads better than a left align option, large and clean input areas, clear transition between login and sign up and easy progression through the form.
